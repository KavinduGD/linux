# Linux vs Unix

The best way to think of the relationship between Linux and Unix is as a **descendant vs. an ancestor**. While they look and feel very similar, their origins and "under-the-hood" philosophies are quite different.

---

## 1. The Core Difference: DNA vs. Imitation

- **UNIX:** Originally developed at **AT&T Bell Labs** in the late 1960s. It is a proprietary system, meaning you usually have to pay for a license to use "Official Unix."
- **Linux:** Created by **Linus Torvalds** in 1991. It was built from scratch to _behave_ like Unix, but it does not contain any of the original Unix code. It is "Unix-like."

---

## 2. Key Comparison

| Feature         | UNIX                                     | Linux                                      |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Philosophy**  | Commercial & Proprietary.                | **Open Source** (GPL License).             |
| **Cost**        | Expensive (License fees).                | **Free** (usually).                        |
| **Development** | Small teams at big companies (IBM, HP).  | Global community (thousands of devs).      |
| **Hardware**    | Often tied to specific high-end servers. | Runs on almost anything (Watches to NASA). |
| **Kernel**      | Monolithic (traditionally).              | Monolithic (but highly modular).           |

---

## 3. The "Free" Factor

The biggest revolution of Linux was the **GPL (General Public License)**.

- If you buy **Unix** (like AIX or HP-UX), you aren't allowed to see or change the source code.
- With **Linux**, you own the code. You can pull it apart, modify it for your specific project (like your ZariAura fashion site or a travel management system), and share it back with the world.

## 4. Where are they now?

- **UNIX** is still used today in "mission-critical" environments like massive banks or specialized medical systems where stability is worth the high cost.
- **Linux** is everywhere. It’s the engine behind **Android**, **AWS**, and almost every web server on the planet.
